    What is SalaryLens?

    SalaryLens is a completely private, browser-based strategic simulation tool for high-earning professionals. It dynamically normalizes your compensation package (base, bonus, equity) against market data to visualize your true leverage before walking into a negotiation.

    Key Features

    • Total Compensation Normalization
    • Visual Negotiation Envelope Mapping
    • 10-Year Compounding Wealth Forecast
    • Auto-Generated AI Talk-Tracks
    • 100% Client-Side Privacy

    How to Use

    1. Input your current compensation package including unvested equity structure.
    2. Set your target ask (the ambitious number you want to achieve).
    3. Research and enter the market bounds for your role (Optional).
    4. Click Analyze to instantly generate your negotiation envelope and walk-away number.
    5. Export your one-page Cheat Sheet to keep on hand during HR/Manager calls.

    Who It's For

    Designed for Tech Executives, Senior Engineers, Directors, and Tier-1 Professionals handling complex job offers involving restricted stock units (RSUs), sign-on bonuses, and varied vesting schedules.

    Why Professionals Need a Salary Negotiation Calculator

    In today's competitive job market across the US, UK, CA, and AU, understanding your true market value is critical. A standard base salary only tells a fraction of the story. For Tier 1 professionals, total compensation (TC) encompasses base pay, target bonuses, and crucially, equity grants like RSUs or stock options. Evaluating a job offer without normalizing these variables leads to money left on the table.

    This free salary negotiation calculator provides a secure, completely private environment to benchmark your compensation against market data. By modeling your "negotiation envelope", you can walk into performance reviews or recruiter calls with extreme confidence and mathematical backing.

    How Total Compensation Normalization Works

    The core logic behind offer evaluation is annualization. To compare a heavy-equity tech offer against a high-base finance offer, everything must be broken down to a 1-year equivalent. Our calculator applies the standard formula: Annual TC = Base Salary + (Base * Bonus %) + (Total Equity / Vesting Years). By applying this uniformly to your current package, the market bounds, and your target ask, you create a direct apples-to-apples comparison.

    Calculating the 10-Year Compounding Impact

    Negotiating a $20,000 raise isn't just about $20,000 today. When invested in the market at a conservative 7% return, compounded alongside standard 3% annual merit increases, that initial bump scales exponentially. The compounding visualizer in this tool demonstrates why the momentary discomfort of negotiating is mathematically worth millions over a decade-long career trajectory.

    Real-World Use Cases

    • Internal Promotions: Benchmarking your current level against external hires to demand equity refreshers.
    • New Job Offers: Evaluating competing offers with different vesting schedules (e.g., 4-year linear vs. 1-year cliff).
    • Performance Reviews: Shifting variable compensation (bonuses) into guaranteed base salary during volatile market conditions.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is this salary negotiation calculator completely free?

    Yes, this tool on Forgenos is 100% free. There is no signup, no subscription, and no hidden fees required to use all features.

    How is Total Compensation (TC) calculated?

    The tool calculates Total Compensation by combining your base salary, your expected annual bonus amount, and the annualized value of your equity grants based on your vesting schedule.

    Is my salary data kept private?

    Absolutely. This tool operates 100% locally in your web browser. Your compensation inputs are never sent to a server or stored in any database.

    How does the 10-year compounding impact work?

    It calculates the difference between your current pay and your target offer, then forecasts how much that extra money would grow over 10 years if invested at a standard market return, alongside standard annual merit raises.

    Can I use this tool on a mobile device?

    Yes, SalaryLens is fully responsive and designed to work seamlessly on smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers without losing functionality.
