About FireCalc

FireCalc is an advanced, privacy-first Financial Independence and Early Retirement (FIRE) modeling terminal. It calculates your exact wealth targets using the 4% Safe Withdrawal Rule, projects lifetime compound growth, and runs intensive Monte Carlo simulations entirely in your browser.

Key Features

  • 4 distinct FIRE modes (Lean, Fat, etc.)
  • 200-path Monte Carlo probability

Comprehensive Guide: How the Calculations Work

At the core of FireCalc is the internationally recognized 4% Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR), derived from the famous Trinity Study. This rule suggests that if you withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually, adjusted for inflation, your money has a high statistical probability of lasting 30 years or more.

  • FIRE Number Formula: Annual Expected Expenses ÷ Safe Withdrawal Rate (e.g., $40,000 ÷ 0.04 = $1,000,000).
  • Real Return: We calculate inflation-adjusted growth using the formula: ((1 + Nominal Return) / (1 + Inflation) - 1). This ensures your projected wealth is represented in today's purchasing power.
  • Monte Carlo Simulation: Instead of assuming a flat 7% return every year (which never happens in reality), our simulation uses the Box-Muller transform to generate randomized returns based on historical stock market volatility (15% standard deviation). We run 200 distinct 30-year scenarios to find your true "survival probability."

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is this financial independence tool completely free?

Yes. FireCalc is a premium-grade free online tool provided by Forgenos. There are no paywalls, hidden fees, or premium versions. You get full access to the Monte Carlo engine instantly.

How accurate is the retirement date projection?

The projection is mathematically precise based on the inputs provided. However, real-world market returns are volatile. We highly recommend using the Monte Carlo simulation feature to stress-test your plan against bad market sequences.

Is my financial data safe here?

100% safe. FireCalc is built entirely on client-side JavaScript. This means your net worth, income, and expenses are processed locally in your browser memory and are never transmitted over the internet or saved to a database.

What does "Fat FIRE" mean?

Fat FIRE refers to retiring early with a large enough portfolio to sustain a luxurious or above-average lifestyle without strict budgeting. It typically requires a much larger FIRE number and utilizes a lower, safer withdrawal rate (like 3%).
