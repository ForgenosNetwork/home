    Why You Need a Debt Payoff Strategy

    Paying minimums on multiple debts is mathematically designed to keep you trapped for decades. The Forgenos DebtSlayer is an advanced, privacy-first command center that shifts power back to you. By applying proven methodologies like the Avalanche and Snowball methods, you visualize the exact day you become debt-free and see exactly how much money you save in interest. Designed for Tier 1 professionals, this tool calculates complex amortization schedules entirely in your browser—ensuring your sensitive financial data remains strictly local and private.

    Core Features

    • Real-time Amortization: Calculates compound interest instantly without server lag.
    • Strategy Comparison: Compares Avalanche, Snowball, Custom, and Minimums side-by-side.
    • Impact Machine: Live slider shows exactly what $50 or $500 extra does to your timeline.
    • Visual Timeline: SVG-rendered chart showing the curve of your debt destruction.
    • Premium Export: Export your master battle plan as a professional image.
    • Zero Data Collection: 100% client-side calculation. We don't want your data.

    How to Execute Your Payoff Plan

    1. Gather Your Data: You need the current balance, APR (interest rate), and minimum monthly payment for each debt.
    2. Input the Targets: Enter each debt into the command center. Watch the "Daily Bleed" metric to see what debt costs you every 24 hours.
    3. Determine Extra Firepower: Input any extra money you can confidently throw at your debt each month.
    4. Select the Weapon: Choose Avalanche (saves most money) or Snowball (fastest early wins).
    5. Follow the Order: The system will generate a strict, priority-based attack order. Pay minimums on everything, and throw all extra cash at the #1 target.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is this debt payoff calculator completely free?

    Yes, this tool on Forgenos is 100% free. No signup, no subscription, no hidden fees. We believe financial freedom tools should not cost money.

    What is the difference between Avalanche and Snowball?

    The Avalanche method mathematically prioritizes your highest interest rate debts first. This saves the absolute most money over time. The Snowball method prioritizes your smallest balance first, regardless of interest. This provides quick psychological wins that research shows helps people stay motivated.

    Is my financial data safe?

    Absolutely. This application runs entirely within your web browser using JavaScript. No databases, no APIs, no tracking. If you close the tab, your data disappears forever.

    What happens if I enter $0 for a minimum payment?

    If a debt has a $0 minimum payment, the interest will still accumulate, causing negative amortization (your balance goes up). The tool handles this math correctly but warns you about the financial danger.
